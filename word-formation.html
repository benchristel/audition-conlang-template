Word Formation

ValdaeanEXONYM has two main parts of speech: nouns and relations. Nouns are like English nouns: they name a person, place, thing, or concept. Relations cover the uses of English adjectives, verbs, and prepositions.

This page covers the rules for forming new words in each of these categories.

Nouns

Compounds

Nouns can be made by compounding two nouns:

  • alososko[pine+forest] "pine-forest" ← alospine "pine" + oskoforest "forest"
  • marovantir[town+guard] "town guard" ← marotown "town" + vantirguard "guard"
  • vantirgon[guard+house] "guard-house" ← vantirguard "guard" + gonhouse "house"

The main noun (which answers the question "what kind of thing is this?") always comes last, as in English.

Nouns can also be formed by prefixing a relation to an existing noun.

  • liryosko[green0+forest] "Greenwood" ← liryagreen0 "green" + oskoforest "forest"

Noun-forming Suffixes

Several suffixes can be added to either nouns or relations to create new nouns.

The suffix -(d)irBLANK#AGT forms a word for a profession or person:

  • keartirgold#AGT "goldsmith" ← keartgold "gold"
  • relabirfarm#AGT "farmer" ← relabfarm "farm"
  • lityadirrun0#AGT "runner" ← lityarun0 "running"

The suffix -lexBLANK#INST forms the names of tools, weapons, and other artificial devices.

  • kallexslay1#INST "sword" ← kalislay1 "slaying, cutting down"
  • warlexlock1#INST "a lock" ← warulock1 "locking"

The suffix -maBLANK#ABST forms words for abstract concepts.

  • aedramatall0#ABST "height" ← aedratall0 "tall"
  • vantmaguard1#ABST "defense" ← vantguard1 "guarding"
  • tialmafriend#ABST "friendship" ← tialfriend "friend"
  • riwaltmaking#ABST "kingship, monarchy" ← riwaltking "king"

Relations

There are also suffixes that create new relations, either from nouns or from existing relations.

The suffix -(a)nnaBLANK#GIVE has the meaning "giving or producing". Relations formed with this suffix are ditransitive: that is, they take both a direct object and an indirect object.

  • leyannaword#GIVE "to say (to someone) (something)" ← leyword "word"
  • intyennaknow1#GIVE "to teach (someone) (something)" ← intyeknow1 "knowing"
  • kalpinnaeat1#GIVE "to feed (someone) (something)" ← kalpieat1 "eating"

The indirect object (the recipient of the action or the thing given) always comes first.

The suffix -iaBLANK#CAUS has a similar meaning, but creates a transitive relation (which takes only a direct object). It can only be applied to relations, not nouns.

  • vuriadie0#CAUS "killing (someone)" ← vurudie0 "dying"
  • kariared0#CAUS "reddening (something)" (e.g. painting it red) ← karyared0 "red"
