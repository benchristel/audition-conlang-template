Relations

As mentioned in the section on word formation, relations are a ValdaeanEXONYM part of speech that subsumes the roles of verbs, adjectives, adverbs, and prepositions in English. A relation either describes a property of something, or describes its relationship to or interaction with another thing.

Modifiers

A relation can modify a noun, as an adjective can in English. A relation used as a modifier simply follows the noun it modifies. The relation keeps its dictionary form.

  • riwaltking aedratall0 "a tall king" (aedratall0 = "tall")
  • oskoforest liryagreen0 "a green forest" (liryagreen0 = "green")

A noun may have multiple relations modifying it. Relations after the first have their first vowel reduplicated and prefixed. If the relation begins with a vowel, an h gets inserted after the prefix.

  • oskoforest aedratall0 i-liryagreen0#TAIL "a tall, green forest"
  • oskoforest liryagreen0 a-haedratall0#TAIL "a green, tall forest"

Some relations act like prepositions, and require a noun to follow them.

  • an riwaltking#DEF vain1 an gonhouse#DEF "the king in the house"

More generally, a relation may have a direct objectâ€”the thing being acted on. The direct object follows the relation.

  • vantirguard kalpieat1 renkbread "a guard (who is) eating bread" (kalpieat1 = "eat", renkbread = "bread")
  • vantirguard aedratall0 a-kalpieat1#TAIL renkbread "a tall guard (who is) eating bread"

Relations can modify other relations, similar to how adverbs and some adjectives can modify adjectives in English.

  • oskoforest liryagreen0 sivnabright0 "a bright green forest (the green is bright)"
  • oskoforest liryagreen0 i-sivnabright0#TAIL "a bright, green forest (the forest is bright)"

Passive Voice

Putting a relation in the passive voice makes its object the subject. The passive voice is formed by suffixing -asBLANK#PASS:

  • kalpaseat1#PASS "being eaten" ← kalpieat1 "eating"

Examples:

  • an vantirguard#DEF kalpieat1 "the guard who is eating"
  • an vantirguard#DEF kalpaseat1#PASS "the guard who is being eaten"

Main Relations

The main relation of a sentence expresses the main action or state the sentence is talking about. It is preceded by a particle azMAIN.

  • An riwaltking#DEF#CAP azMAIN kalpieat1 renkbread. "The king eats bread."
  • An oskoforest#DEF#CAP azMAIN liryagreen0. "The forest is green."

Infinitives

The infinitive form of a relation is formed by suffixing -anBLANK#INF.

  • An riwaltking#DEF#CAP azMAIN naelwant1 kalpianeat1#INF renkbread "The king wants to eat bread."
