Forms of Nouns

Singular

The dictionary form of nouns in ValdaeanEXONYM has no implication of singular or plural. It can be used to refer to one thing or many.

To explicitly make a noun singular, add the suffix -(e)lBLANK#SG.

  • yostohome → yostolhome#SG "a home"
  • vantirguard → vantirelguard#SG "a guard"
  • gonhouse → gonelhouse#SG "a building"
  • marotown → maroltown#SG "a town"
  • alospine → aloselpine#SG "a pine"
  • relabfarm → relabelfarm#SG "a farm"
  • tialfriend → tialelfriend#SG "a friend"
  • riwaltking → riwaltelking#SG "a king"
  • leyword → leyelword#SG "a word"

Nouns ending in -x have singular forms ending in -k:

  • kallexsword → kalleksword#SG "a sword"
  • warlexlock → warleklock#SG "a lock"

Adding -(e)lBLANK#SG to a noun that would be a mass noun (a non-pluralizable noun) in English creates the meaning "a piece of...". For example:

  • keartgold → keartelgold#SG "gold coin" (literally, "a piece of gold")

There is another singular suffix, -ulBLANK#PEJSG, which is used to refer to despised things:

  • vantirguard → vantirulguard#PEJSG "damn guard"
  • gonhouse → gonulhouse#PEJSG "damn house"

Plural

The plural suffix is -(r)imBLANK#PL:

  • yostohome → yostorimhome#PL "homes"
  • vantirguard → vantirimguard#PL "guards"
  • gonhouse → gonimhouse#PL "buildings"
  • marotown → marorimtown#PL "towns"
  • alospine → alosimpine#PL "pines"
  • relabfarm → relabimfarm#PL "farms"
  • tialfriend → tialimfriend#PL "friends"
  • riwaltking → riwaltimking#PL "kings"
  • leyword → leyimword#PL "words"

There is also a plural suffix for referring to despised things:

  • vantirguard → vantirotsguard#PEJPL "damn guards"
  • gonhouse → gonotshouse#PEJPL "damn houses"

Definite

The ValdaeanEXONYM definite article anBLANK#DEF is similar to the English word "the". It marks a noun as referring to something that the listener already knows about.

  • an riwaltking#DEF "the king"
  • an marotown#DEF "the town"
